Dr. René Favaloro

The man who changed the medicine

René Gerónimo Favaloro was an Argentine cardiac surgeon and educator best known for his pioneering work on coronary artery bypass surgery using the great saphenous vein.

  • 1923 - Born in La Plata, Buenos Aires, Argentina
  • 1936 - Admitted into the Colegio Nacional de La Plata (high school)
  • 1949 - Graduated with a medical degree
  • 1951 - Married with Antonia Delgado
  • 1967 - Began to consider the possibility of using the saphenous vein in coronary surgery
  • 1970 - Published one of his best-known volumes, Surgical Treatment of Coronary Arteriosclerosis.
  • 1975 - He founded the Fundación Favaloro with several collaborators
  • 1980 - He established the "Basic Investigation Laboratory" (Laboratorio de Investigación Básica), which was long financed with his own money
  • 1992 - The nonprofit Favaloro Foundation Institute of Cardiology and Cardiovascular Surgery (Instituto de Cardiología y Cirugía Cardiovascular de la Fundación Favaloro) opened in Buenos Aires
  • 2000 - On July 29 Favaloro fatally shot himself in the chest. He was submerged in an economic and political crisis, and the Favaloro Foundation was US$18 million in debt.

"The advances in medicine and bioengineering can be considered true achievements for humanity when all people have access to their benefits and cease to be a privilege for minorities."

Dr. René Favaloro
